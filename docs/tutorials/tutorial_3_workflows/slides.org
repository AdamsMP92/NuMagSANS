* Goal
Generate one spherical object and compare several local magnetization models.

- The script writes one shared local sphere.
- Each parameter set writes one file ~m_i.csv~ in ~MagData/Object_1~.
- Static PNG diagnostics are written for quick inspection and slides.

* Spherical Vector-Field Library
The spherical library combines a geometry, a radial profile, and a field ansatz.

#+begin_export latex
\scriptsize
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\textbf{field\_type} & \textbf{coordinate input} & \textbf{profile} & \textbf{main parameters}\\
\hline
\texttt{vortex} & \(\rho,\varphi\) & \(\alpha(\xi)\) & \texttt{xi\_type}, \texttt{profile\_type}, \texttt{kappa}, \texttt{turns}, \texttt{normalize}\\
\texttt{hedgehog} & \(r,\theta,\varphi\) & \(\alpha(\xi)\) & \texttt{xi\_type}, \texttt{profile\_type}, \texttt{kappa}, \texttt{turns}, \texttt{normalize}\\
\texttt{artichoke} & \(r,\theta,\varphi\) & \(\alpha(\xi)\) & \texttt{xi\_type}, \texttt{profile\_type}, \texttt{kappa}, \texttt{turns}, \texttt{normalize}\\
\texttt{poloidal\_vortex} & \(r,\theta,\varphi\) & \(\alpha(\xi)\) & \texttt{xi\_type}, \texttt{profile\_type}, \texttt{kappa}, \texttt{turns}, \texttt{normalize}\\
\texttt{skyrmion} & \(\rho,\varphi\) & built-in & \texttt{N}, \texttt{m}, \texttt{gamma}, \texttt{xi\_type}\\
\texttt{transversal\_helix} & \(z\) & none & \texttt{k}, \texttt{chirality}, \texttt{phase}\\
\texttt{longitudinal\_helix} & \(z\) & none & \texttt{k}, \texttt{chirality}, \texttt{phase}, \texttt{lambda}\\
\texttt{linearized\_vortex} & \(x,y\) & none & \texttt{m\_0}, \texttt{m\_1}\\
\end{tabular}
#+end_export

- The profile-based fields share the same \(\alpha(\xi)\) interface.
- ~normalize=True~ normalizes the final vector field; set ~normalize=False~ to keep raw amplitudes.

* Profile-Based Spherical Fields
For the profile-based fields, the same radial tilt is inserted into different bases.

#+begin_export latex
\begingroup
\small
\[
\xi =
\begin{cases}
\rho/R, & \texttt{cylindrical\_xi}\\
r/R, & \texttt{spherical\_xi}
\end{cases}
\qquad
\alpha=\alpha(\xi)
\]
\endgroup
\begingroup
\footnotesize
\[
\begin{array}{rl}
\texttt{vortex}: & \sin\alpha\,\mathbf{e}_{\varphi}+\cos\alpha\,\mathbf{e}_z\\
\texttt{hedgehog}: & \sin\alpha\cos\theta\,\mathbf{e}_r+\cos\alpha\,\mathbf{e}_z\\
\texttt{artichoke}: & -\sin\alpha\,\mathbf{e}_{\theta}+\cos\alpha\,\mathbf{e}_z\\
\texttt{poloidal\_vortex}: & \sin\alpha\,\mathbf{e}_{\theta}+\cos\alpha\,\mathbf{e}_z
\end{array}
\]
\endgroup
\begin{center}
\scriptsize
\begin{tabular}{llll}
\texttt{linear} & \texttt{parabolic} & \texttt{trigonometric} & \texttt{hyperbolic}\\
\texttt{argument\_hyperbolic} & \texttt{lorentzian} & \texttt{gaussian} & \texttt{arctan}
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
#+end_export

- ~turns~ multiplies \(\alpha\) before the basis components are assembled.
- ~normalize=True~ normalizes the result after the field is evaluated.

* Non-Profile Spherical Fields
Some spherical-library fields define the vector components directly.

#+begin_export latex
\footnotesize
\[
\begin{array}{rll}
\texttt{skyrmion}: &
\left(\sin(\pi N\xi)\cos(m\varphi+\gamma),
\sin(\pi N\xi)\sin(m\varphi+\gamma),
\cos(\pi N\xi)\right)
& N,m,\gamma\\[0.3ex]
\texttt{transversal\_helix}: &
\mathbf{e}_x\cos(kz+\varphi_0)+
\chi\,\mathbf{e}_y\sin(kz+\varphi_0)
& k,\chi,\varphi_0\\[0.3ex]
\texttt{longitudinal\_helix}: &
\mathbf{m}'_{\mathrm{helix}}+\lambda\,\mathbf{e}_z,\quad
\mathbf{m}=\mathbf{m}'/|\mathbf{m}'|
& k,\chi,\varphi_0,\lambda\\[0.3ex]
\texttt{linearized\_vortex}: &
(-m_1y,\;m_1x,\;m_0)
& m_0,m_1
\end{array}
\]
#+end_export

- Useful when the texture is not primarily controlled by a radial tilt profile.
- The final normalization can be disabled with ~normalize=False~.

* Operator-Kernel Approach
The operator-kernel library builds each component from a differential operator applied to a scalar kernel.

#+begin_export latex
\[
\mathbf{m}'(\mathbf{r}) =
\begin{pmatrix}
P_x(\partial_x,\partial_y,\partial_z)K(\mathbf{r})\\
P_y(\partial_x,\partial_y,\partial_z)K(\mathbf{r})\\
P_z(\partial_x,\partial_y,\partial_z)K(\mathbf{r})
\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
\mathbf{m}=\mathbf{m}'/|\mathbf{m}'|
\]
#+end_export

- \(P_x,P_y,P_z\) are polynomials in the derivative symbols \(\partial_x,\partial_y,\partial_z\).
- ~component_specs~ stores these three polynomials as strings in ~dx~, ~dy~, ~dz~.
- In Fourier space, derivatives correspond to multipliers \(i q_x,i q_y,i q_z\).
- ~normalize=True~ normalizes \(\mathbf{m}'\) after all components are evaluated.

* Operator Specifications
Example component specifications for a vortex-like operator-kernel field.

#+begin_export latex
\[
\begin{array}{rclcl}
\texttt{"-a * dy"} &\Rightarrow& P_x=-a\,\partial_y
&\Rightarrow& m_x'=-a\,\partial_y K\\
\texttt{"a * dx"} &\Rightarrow& P_y=a\,\partial_x
&\Rightarrow& m_y'=a\,\partial_x K\\
\texttt{"c"} &\Rightarrow& P_z=c
&\Rightarrow& m_z'=c\,K
\end{array}
\]
#+end_export

- Higher derivatives are possible, e.g. ~dx^2~, ~dy^2~, or mixed operator polynomials.
- Coefficients such as \(a,c,d\) are supplied through ~operator_parameters~.
- The raw field can be kept with ~normalize=False~.

* Gaussian and Sech Kernels
The kernel controls the localization; the component operators control the vector texture.

#+begin_export latex
\[
K_{\mathrm{Gaussian}}(\mathbf{r})
\propto
\exp\!\left[-\frac{1}{2}
\mathbf{r}^\mathsf{T}
\Sigma^{-1}
\mathbf{r}
\right]
\]
\[
K_{\mathrm{sech}}(\mathbf{r})
=
\prod_{i=x,y,z}\frac{a_i/\pi}{\cosh(a_i r_i)}
\]
#+end_export

- ~kernel_type="gaussian"~ uses ~sigma~ or a full covariance matrix ~Sigma~.
- ~kernel_type="sech"~ uses one width parameter per coordinate direction.
- ~coordinate_scale~ rescales object coordinates before evaluating the kernel.

* Common Profile
The first four fields use a hyperbolic tilt profile.

#+begin_export latex
\[
\alpha(\xi)
=
\arccos\!\left(\sqrt{1-\tanh^2(\kappa \xi)}\right),
\qquad \kappa = 6,\quad \mathrm{turns}=1 .
\]
#+end_export

- ~cylindrical_xi~ uses \(\xi=\rho/R\).
- ~spherical_xi~ uses \(\xi=r/R\).
